Multi-tenancy in ThingsBoard enables a single platform instance to securely serve multiple tenants.

A **Tenant** is a top-level logical entity that represents an organizational boundary (for example, an independent business entity, company, customer segment, or business unit) and encapsulates all related IoT entities and configurations, including devices, assets, dashboards, rule chains, users, and other tenant-specific resources.

## Isolation and security

Multi-tenancy in ThingsBoard is implemented through strict logical isolation:

<ul>
  <li>Each entity belongs to exactly one tenant.</li>
  <li>Permissions are enforced by <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/roles/' target="_blank">role-based access control</DocLink>.</li>
  <li>Tenant Administrators cannot access resources belonging to other tenants.</li>
  {![Products.PAAS, Products.PAAS_EU].includes(props.product) && <li>System Administrators have global visibility across all tenants.</li>}
</ul>

This ensures secure tenant data isolation, even when multiple tenants operate within the same platform instance while sharing the underlying infrastructure.

<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="tenant-management" showFor="ce,pe">Tenant management</ConditionalHeading>

Tenants are created and managed by the **system administrator**.

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="create-tenant" showFor="ce,pe">Create tenant</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
1. Log in to ThingsBoard as a **System administrator**.
2. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
3. Click the **+ Add tenant** in the top right corner.
4. Enter a **title**.
5. Use the default <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/tenant-profiles' target="_blank">tenant profile</DocLink> or select a different one.
6. Optionally, provide an address, phone number, contact email.
7. Click **Add**.
</Steps>

Once created, the tenant becomes available in the tenants list.

---

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="edit-tenant" showFor="ce,pe">Edit tenant</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
2. Click the tenant row to open its details.
3. Click the orange **pencil** icon (**Edit**) to enter edit mode.
4. Update the tenant information.
5. **Apply changes**.
</Steps>

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="delete-tenant" showFor="ce,pe">Delete tenant</ConditionalHeading>

Click the **trash** icon next to the tenant and confirm deleting, or
open the tenant details page and click **Delete tenant**.

<Aside type="danger" title="Warning">
  Deleting a tenant removes all tenant-related data and entities.
</Aside>

---

<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="tenant-administrator-management" showFor="ce,pe">Tenant administrator management</ConditionalHeading>

**Tenant administrator** is a user with full administrative permissions who manages all tenant-scoped entities and configuration.
All operations are isolated within the tenant context and do not affect other tenants in the system.

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="create-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Create tenant administrator</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to the **Tenants**.
2. In the **Tenants** list, click **Manage tenant admins** for **Tenant**.
3. Click **+ Add user** in the top right corner.
4. Enter the user&#39;s **email address**.
  Optionally, provide first and last name, and phone number.
5. Choose the user activation method:
    - **Display activation link** — generates an activation link that you can copy and share manually.
    - **Send activation mail** — sends the activation link to the specified email address (requires a configured <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/ui/mail-settings' target="_blank">mail server</DocLink>).
6. Click **Add**.
</Steps>

After creation, the tenant administrator can log in and manage tenant resources.

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="edit-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Edit tenant administrator</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
  1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
  2. Click **Manage tenant admins** for **Tenant**.
  3. Click the Tenant administrator row to open its details.
  4. Click the orange **pencil** icon (**Edit**) to enter edit mode.
  5. Update the tenant information.
  6. Apply changes.
</Steps>

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="enabledisable-user-account" showFor="ce,pe">Enable/disable user account</ConditionalHeading>

ThingsBoard allows administrators to temporarily block a user's access to the platform without deleting the account.
Disabled users cannot log in or access any tenant resources.

To enable or disable a user account:

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
2. Click **Manage tenant admins** for desired Tenant.
3. Click the User row to open its details.
4. Click **Enable/Disable User Account**.
</Steps>

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="delete-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Delete tenant administrator</ConditionalHeading>

Click the **trash** icon next to the tenant and confirm deleting, or
open the tenant details page and click **Delete tenant**.

<Aside type="danger" title="Warning">
  Deleting a tenant removes all tenant-related data and entities.
</Aside>

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="login-as-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Login as tenant administrator</ConditionalHeading>

System Administrators can log in as a Tenant Administrator directly from the tenants list by using the **Login as tenant admin** action.

This is useful for troubleshooting tenant-specific issues and verifying tenant configuration.

<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="best-practices" showFor="ce,pe">Best practices</ConditionalHeading>

To ensure stable and secure multi-tenant deployments:
- Use Tenant Profiles to enforce quotas and prevent excessive resource usage.
- Assign at least two Tenant Administrators per tenant for redundancy.
- Use consistent naming conventions for tenants and customer structures.
- Monitor message throughput and entity limits for each tenant.
