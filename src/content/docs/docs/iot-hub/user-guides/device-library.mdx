The **Device Library** is a catalog of pre-built IoT device connectors in ThingsBoard's **IoT Hub**, contributed by verified hardware vendors. It lets you connect supported devices with minimal manual configuration — select a device from the catalog, click **Connect device**, and a guided wizard automatically provisions everything your device needs in your ThingsBoard instance.

Depending on the device, connecting it creates a <DocLink product={Products.CE} path="user-guide/devices/" target="_blank">Device</DocLink>, <DocLink product={Products.CE} path="user-guide/device-profiles/" target="_blank">Device Profile</DocLink>, and <DocLink product={Products.CE} path="user-guide/dashboards/" target="_blank">Dashboard</DocLink>. Devices connected through a network server may also provision a <DocLink product={Products.GW} path="what-is-thingsboard-iot-gateway/" target="_blank">Gateway</DocLink>, <DocLink product={Products.CE} path="user-guide/integrations/" target="_blank">Integration</DocLink>, and <DocLink product={Products.CE} path="user-guide/integrations/uplink-data-converter/" target="_blank">Uplink Converter</DocLink>.

## Connecting a Device

To open the Device Library, click **IoT Hub** in the ThingsBoard left sidebar and select **Device Library**.

<Steps>
  1. Browse the catalog and find your device.
  2. Click the device card to open its detail panel and review the connection method, prerequisites, and setup notes.
  3. Click **Connect device** to start the setup wizard.
  4. **Connection method** *(shown only for network-server-connected devices)* — Select the network server your device connects through. Devices that connect directly (MQTT, HTTP, CoAP) skip this step.
  5. **Prerequisites** — Review the hardware, software, and account setup required before proceeding. The wizard links to user manuals, required gateways, or software that must be installed first.
  6. **Configuration** — Fill in device name and any connection-specific details such as network server credentials, gateway address, or broker settings. The exact fields depend on the device and connection method.
  7. **Provisioning** — The wizard creates all required entities in your ThingsBoard instance and shows the status of each item. Wait until all items show **Done**.
  8. **Final step** — Follow the post-setup instructions shown (such as an HTTP endpoint URL to register in your network server, a docker compose command to launch a gateway, or a configuration table to apply to the physical device), then click **Open Dashboard** or **Open Device**.
</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
  If data is not appearing after setup, check the troubleshooting notes on the final wizard step, or re-read the device state through your network server or gateway interface to force a data refresh.
</Aside>

## Removing a Device

Click the **Installed Items** button at the top of the Device Library to open the list of installed devices. Click the **Remove** icon next to the device you want to remove and confirm the action. 

<Aside type="caution">
  This will permanently delete the device and its associated resources from your tenant.
</Aside>

## Checking for Updates

Click the **Installed Items** button at the top of the Device Library, then click **Check for updates** in the top-right corner. ThingsBoard compares all installed items against the latest versions and adds an **Updates** column showing either **Up to date** or an available version to install.
