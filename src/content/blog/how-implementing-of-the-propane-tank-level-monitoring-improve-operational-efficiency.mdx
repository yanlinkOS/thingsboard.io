## What is propane tank monitoring?

![](/images/blog/how-implementing-of-the-propane-tank-level-monitoring-improve-operational-efficiency/tank-level-20.webp)

A propane tank level monitoring system is a specialized system used to monitor and control the amount of propane in one or more tanks. This system can be used for both domestic and commercial applications, for example, by propane suppliers where propane storage is a critical component of the operational process. This system consists of three main components:

- Sensors to accurately detect the propane level;
- Communication network to transmit the data from sensors to the platform;
- Platforms to store, process, and display the data.

The main idea of the propane tank monitoring system is to remotely monitor tank levels 24/7 using tank telemetry and see in real-time how much propane is in the tank, helping better manage propane and keep business running smoothly. Propane tank monitoring is accomplished through the use of smart technology, such as a wireless propane tank level monitor and float gauge, as well as reliable communication methods to deliver the measurements to the cloud system. With the right IoT platform and its easy-to-use dashboard, users can quickly get all the information about propane tanks or check levels via smartphone or PC. Efficiently managing propane inventory and being notified about any sensor alerts, helps avoid interruptions and ensure having propane when needing it, and even improves business sustainability and environmental compliance by detecting leaks in the tanks, making operations more efficient and customers happier.

In addition, the tank level monitoring system can be adapted to the needs of the business, for example, in sales, manufacturing or any other industry where propane levels need to be closely monitored. The tank level monitoring system helps to make business more efficient by using resources more accurately and carefully.

## How propane tank level monitoring system works?

![](/images/blog/how-implementing-of-the-propane-tank-level-monitoring-improve-operational-efficiency/tank-level-21.webp)

There are many propane level sensors, such as an external magnetic sensor, float gauge, and remote gauges for vertical propane tanks or horizontal propane tanks. They can be installed indoors or outdoors, and they all perform the same function – read the remaining contents of your propane tank. The sensor is mounted on the lid of a closed tank or suspended by a rod or rope in the tank, or a float gauge is used. Once installed, the sensor can check the amount of liquid in the tank. To do this, it sends a wave through the liquid and measures the time it takes for the wave to reflect off the surface of the liquid. The device collects this information from the sensor and sends it to our IoT visualization platform for tracking real-time tank level. This gives you the ability to monitor propane levels remotely from anywhere in the world via a web or smartphone app, making it easy to keep track of the amount of liquid in any tank.

## Wireless devices for propane tank monitoring

![](/images/blog/how-implementing-of-the-propane-tank-level-monitoring-improve-operational-efficiency/tank-level-22-1.webp)

Wireless devices for remote propane tank monitoring have many advantages over standard tank level measurement devices. They can be used to remotely control and monitor the level of propane in tanks. These devices use a variety of sensors and communication technologies to transmit data on propane level, temperature, and sometimes even propane quality to a central system or directly to users via our platform in real-time. Here are some of the benefits and specifications of IoT devices used for wireless propane tank monitoring:

1. **Level sensors:** These sensors measure the amount of propane in the tank for the accurate and repeatable tank level readings. The technologies used include ultrasonic, radar, and float sensors, each with its own advantages and suitable for different tank conditions – vertical propane tank or horizontal propane tank, It can be installed indoors or outdoors.
2. **Temperature sensors:** Remote reading of propane tank contents helps to measure temperature, it is vital for for business and farming, as changes in temperature can affect volume and quality of propane.
3. **Connectivity:** These devices often use wireless technology such as Wi-Fi, LoRaWAN, NB-IoT or cellular networks to transmit data to a IoT platform. This allows for remote monitoring and alerting.
4. **Power supply:** Many IoT devices for propane tank monitoring are designed with energy efficiency in mind. They can use batteries, solar power, or energy harvesting technologies to operate in remote or hard-to-reach locations without requiring frequent maintenance, what is very important for tanks in the field, tanks below ground, and other tanks located in the hard-to-reach places.

## Residential propane tank monitoring & commercial propane tank monitoring

### Commercial propane tank monitoring

![](/images/blog/how-implementing-of-the-propane-tank-level-monitoring-improve-operational-efficiency/tank-level-26.webp)

Tank monitoring in the oil, gas, and energy for business and farming encompasses a wide range of technologies to optimize the storage, use, and distribution of various propane and bulk storage. This sector requires accurate and reliable monitoring solutions due to the high volumes, potential environmental and safety risks, and economic implications of storage and distribution. Benefits of tank monitoring in the oil and energy industry:
Operational Efficiency: Real-time monitoring allows for just-in-time delivery and distribution, reducing holding costs and optimizing supply chains.
Safety and Environmental Protection: Early detection of leaks and potential hazards minimizes the risk of accidents and environmental damage.
Cost Savings: By optimizing inventory management and reducing the risk of spillage or contamination, companies can achieve significant cost savings.
**Data-Driven Decisions**: Access to detailed analytics and trends enables better strategic planning and investment decisions

### Residential propane tank monitoring

![](/images/blog/how-implementing-of-the-propane-tank-level-monitoring-improve-operational-efficiency/tank-level-27.webp)

Residential propane tank monitoring refers to the use of technology to continuously measure and track the propane levels in a household propane tank. This system typically involves sensors placed for each tank, which transmit data to a monitoring device or a cloud-based platform. Homeowners and service providers can access this data in real time through mobile apps, web portals, or notification systems. Benefits of residential propane tank monitoring:
**Convenience:** Eliminates the need to manually check the propane tank level gauge, especially in adverse weather conditions or for tanks located in hard-to-reach areas.
**Cost savings:** By understanding consumption patterns and ordering propane only when needed, homeowners can avoid unnecessary deliveries and take advantage of lower prices during off-peak periods.
Environmental impact: Effective monitoring and tracking of consumption can reduce energy consumption and carbon dioxide emissions by optimizing heating efficiency.
